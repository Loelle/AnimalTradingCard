Groundhog

The groundhogs are excellent diggers, and construct elaborated burrows with minimum two entrances. They hibernate during the winter. When they get out from their burrows, it announces that the winter is over, and extreme temperatures belong to the past.

  • Scientific Name: Marmota monax
  • Average Length: 40 to 65 centimeters (16 to 26 in.)
  • Average Lifespan: 6 years (in the wild)
  • Habitat: Burrows and undercover in forests and grassy patures

The groundhog is a rodent belonging to the large group of squirrels. They are widely distributed in North America. Groundhogs are accomplished swimmer, and occasionnally climb trees when they want to survey their surrounding or to escape predators. However, they prefer to retreat to their burrows when they are threatened. Mostly herbivorous, they also occasionnally eat grubs, insects and snails.

Marmots are very fearful. But, when you are lucky enough to be able to observe them without being scared, you will discover that they spend most of their free time playing with each other.
